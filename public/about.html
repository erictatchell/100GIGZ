100GIGZ is a private media archive that allows invited members to securely view, upload, organize, comment on, and interact with shared photos and videos. Google Sign-In is used to authenticate members and display their basic profile identity.

What 100GIGZ Is

100GIGZ was created as a private place for preserving shared memories, trips, videos, photos, and other media in an organized archive.

The service is not a public social network. Access is limited to approved or invited members, and archive content is intended only for the people authorized to view it.

What Members Can Do

  • Sign in securely using a Google account.
  • View photos, videos, posts, trips, and archive collections.
  • Upload and organize media where permission is provided.
  • Maintain a member profile within the archive.
  • Like, comment on, and interact with shared content.

Google Sign-In

Google Sign-In is used to authenticate members and provide basic account identity information, such as a member's name, email address, and profile photo.

Google account information is used only to support authentication, member identification, account access, and profile display inside 100GIGZ.

100GIGZ does not use Google user data for advertising and does not sell personal information.

Access and Security

100GIGZ uses layered access controls designed to prevent archive content from being publicly accessible. These controls may include a vault access key, authenticated member accounts, account permissions, and Firebase security rules.
